My Favorite TV Shows




Superheroes

Superheroes-Action

The Flash

The Flash is a superhero TV series that follows the adventures of Barry Allen, a forensic scientist who gains superhuman speed after a freak accident. Using his new powers, Barry fights crime and protects Central City from various villains.

Director: David Nutter, Glen Winter, and Kevin Smiths

Stars: Grant Gustin as Barry Allen/The Flash, Candice Patton as Iris West-Allen, Danielle Panabaker as Caitlin Snow/Killer Frost, Carlos Valdes as Cisco Ramon/Vibe, Jesse L. Martin as Joe West

Superheroes-Drama

WandaVision

The show is set after the events of "Avengers: Endgame" and follows Wanda Maximoff (aka Scarlet Witch) and Vision as they try to live a suburban life in the town of Westview. As the show progresses, it becomes clear that all is not as it seems, and Wanda's powers are at play in creating this alternate reality.

Director: Jac Schaeffer and Matt Shakman

Stars: Elizabeth Olsen as Wanda Maximoff/Scarlet Witch, Paul Bettany as Vision. Kathryn Hahn as Agatha Harkness, Teyonah Parris as Monica Rambeau, Randall Park as Jimmy Woo

Superheroes-Action

Titans

Titans is a live-action superhero television series based on the DC Comics team of the same name. The show follows a group of young superheroes led by Batman's former sidekick, Dick Grayson (Robin/Nightwing), as they combat evil and face their own personal struggles.

Director: Brad Anderson, Kevin Rodney Sullivan and Toa Fraser

Stars: Brenton Thwaites as Dick Grayson/Robin/Nightwing, Anna Diop as Koriand'r/Starfire, Teagan Croft as Rachel Roth/Raven, Minka Kelly as Dawn Granger/Dove




Sitcom

Sitcom

Big Bang Theory

The Big Bang Theory is a sitcom that revolves around a group of brilliant but socially awkward physicists and their neighbor, an attractive aspiring actress. The show explores their daily lives, their relationships, and their various interests and passions, from comic books and science fiction to video games and Dungeons and Dragons.

Director: Chuck Lorre and Bill Prady

Stars: Johnny Galecki as Leonard Hofstadter, Jim Parsons as Sheldon Cooper, Kaley Cuoco as Penny, Simon Helberg as Howard Wolowitz, Kunal Nayyar as Raj Koothrappali

Sitcom

Modern Family

The show revolves around the lives of three different families, all related to each other through Jay Pritchett, his children, and their respective partners and children. The families deal with various issues and situations, including family dynamics, relationships, and life events.

Director: Jason Winer, Steven Levitan, and James Bagdonas

Stars: Ed O'Neill as Jay Pritchett, Sofía Vergara as Gloria Delgado-Pritchett, Julie Bowen as Claire Dunphy, Ty Burrell as Phil Dunphy, Jesse Tyler Ferguson as Mitchell Pritchett

Sitcom

Young Sheldon

Young Sheldon is a comedy television series that serves as a prequel to the popular show "The Big Bang Theory." The show follows the childhood of Sheldon Cooper, a brilliant and socially awkward physicist, growing up in East Texas during the 1980s. The series explores Sheldon's relationship with his family, including his twin sister and parents, as well as his struggles to fit in with his peers.

Director: Jon Favreau and Jaffar Mahmood

Stars: Iain Armitage as Sheldon Cooper, Zoe Perry as Mary Cooper, Lance Barber as George Cooper Sr.

The Sandman

"The Sandman" is a fantasy drama TV series based on the comic book series of the same name by Neil Gaiman. It follows the story of Morpheus, the Lord of Dreams, who is imprisoned for 70 years and seeks to regain his lost power and rebuild his kingdom, the Dreaming.

Director: Allan Heinberg and Gaiman

Stars: Tom Sturridge as Dream/Morpheus, Gwendoline Christie as Lucifer Morningstar, Charles Dance as Roderick Burgess, Boyd Holbrook as The Corinthian, Kirby Howell-Baptiste as Death
