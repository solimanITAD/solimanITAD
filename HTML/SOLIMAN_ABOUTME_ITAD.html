Childhood Years

          As a child, all I did was play, eat, and sleep.1 One of the fondest memories of my childhood was whenever it was Christmas time.2 My parents will always buy me a present and I would always pick a toy car, whether it’s a remote controlled one or just a plain car toy.3 I remember being obsessed with toys especially when it’s car related.4 I actually still keep some of the toys that my parents gave me back then cause it holds so much memory to me.5 One of the happy car-related memories I had when I was a child was when my cousin who came from the province visited me.6 Her sister bought him a remote controlled car and we competed with each other to see which car is the fastest between the two.7 He actually won as I discovered that it actually depends on what battery the car is using when I took a look at the battery of his car, his sister bought him the energizer battery while I use eveready.8 When I was a kindergarten student, I almost didn’t make it to Grade 1 because of my age.9 I started school early only when I was 4 years old.10 Back in the day, that was early already.11 The deciding factor for me to be able to pass kindergarten was I should be able to read words.12 Thankfully I passed with flying colors as my mother would say.13 During my elementary days, I can say that I almost participated in all of the activities.14 There was a funny story when I was a Grade 1 student.15 In public schools when a teacher is absent, the class that she was an adviser to gets transferred to another section temporarily for the whole day.16 One time, my adviser was absent, my classmates and I got separated into another section.17 You wanna know what the funny part is? Me and one of my classmates snuck out and just stayed outside of the rooms.18 The reason why I snuck out was because I will get transferred to a teacher that I was really scared of.19 I remember crying and crying until a teacher noticed us.20 She decided to call my mother who immediately fetched me and we just went home for the day.21

          High school was where life got more serious to me, academically.1 Another story worth telling and I would say if this incident didn’t happen I won’t be standing here today.2 So the story begins with my aunt enrolling me for Grade 7.3 My mother asked my aunt to enroll me since we will be out of town during the enrollment day.4 We went to Iloilo to visit my mother’s family.5 During that time I was so excited because I got to ride an airplane on the way there.6 I’m not gonna lie, I was nervous at that time since I don’t know how it feels and I’ve seen the movie 2012 which kind of traumatized me.7 What I liked the most when we were there was the freshness of the air.8 The lack of signal was also a good break from social media.9 When we want to get a signal, we need to get to the highest part of the place.10 While we we’re having fun enjoying our vacation, our aunt suddenly texted and told my mother that I was needed in the enrollment since there was an interview to be conducted.11 My mother was confused about it since my sisters who are also currently enrolled didn’t go through the enrollment process.12 As a result of that our vacation has come to an end for only a week. On our way home we took a RORO which took almost 2 days compared to the airplane which only took at least 1-2 hours.13 As we got home, we found out that my aunt enrolled me in a section that specializes in STEM.14 If I haven’t mentioned yet I went to Rizal High School in Pasig City which has been listed as the largest secondary school in the world since 1993 by the Guinness Book of World Records until 2005 .15 Each year there consists of like 30+ sections and my aunt enrolled me in STEM.16 Thankfully I passed the grade requirements but sadly, in the interview I failed.17 At the end of the day, I got put in the pilot section.18 As the school year began, I was pushing myself to do good in every aspect of life.19 I was shocked after our 1st grading. I was included in the Top 10 of the class.20 Little did I know that small decision that my aunt did, made a big impact in my life.21 I can proudly say that I was always included in the top of my class.22 Sadly, she passed away a few years ago and she will always have a special place in my heart as the person who believed in me no matter what.23
